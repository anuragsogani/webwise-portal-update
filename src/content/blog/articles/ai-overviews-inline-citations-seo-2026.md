## Introduction: search has fundamentally changed  -  again

Google's May 2026 AI Overviews update is the most significant change to search since the introduction of featured snippets. Inline citations, enhanced source preview cards, and a dedicated "Expert Advice" block have collectively dismantled the assumption that ranking equals visibility.

For enterprise brands and their marketing teams, the implications are structural. The traditional funnel  -  rank, earn clicks, convert on-site  -  is no longer the only path. AI Overviews now answer queries directly, with source attribution that users may never click. The brands that thrive are those cited within the AI-generated answer itself.

This article examines what changed in May 2026, what it means for SEO strategy, and how to optimise for both traditional search and generative AI surfaces simultaneously.

---

## What changed in May 2026

### Inline citations replace pill stacks

Before May 2026, AI Overview source links appeared as small "pill" icons stacked at the bottom of the generated answer. Users had to scroll past the answer to find them. The May update moved citations inline  -  directly next to the specific text, bullet point, or statistic each source supports.

This is a fundamental shift in attribution visibility. Brands cited in AI Overviews are now visible at the point of consumption, not as an afterthought. Conversely, brands that are not cited are now entirely invisible on these pages  -  there is no ambient visibility from being "somewhere on the page."

### Enhanced preview cards on hover

On desktop, hovering over an inline citation now displays a preview card showing the site name, page title, and a brief content extract. This means brand recognition  -  not just content quality  -  directly affects whether users click through.

A recognisable brand name in a preview card earns clicks. A generic or unknown brand name does not. Entity clarity and brand authority have become click-through rate factors in a way that was previously limited to traditional SERP snippets.

### Expert Advice blocks

The most significant addition is the "Expert Advice" section within AI Overviews. This block specifically pulls first-hand perspectives, original research, and experiential content from forums, social media, review sites, and authoritative blogs.

Google's documentation explicitly states that this feature prioritises content demonstrating first-hand experience  -  the "first E" in E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness). Generic, fact-aggregated content  -  the kind that could be written by anyone without domain expertise  -  is systematically excluded from Expert Advice blocks.

### Multimodal, conversational search inputs

The redesigned search box now accepts images, files, and longer conversational queries. Users are increasingly typing questions the way they would ask a colleague, not the way they would construct a keyword search. This accelerates the shift toward natural-language, intent-driven content that answers specific questions directly.

## The decoupling of rank and citation

The most consequential finding from May 2026 data is the collapse in correlation between traditional organic ranking and AI Overview citation.

Before this update, there was a reasonable (though imperfect) correlation: pages ranking in the top 3–5 organic positions were disproportionately cited in AI Overviews. The May 2026 data shows this correlation has weakened significantly. Google's systems now evaluate "source-worthiness" independently of traditional ranking signals.

**What source-worthiness means in practice:**

- **Original data and research:** Pages with proprietary data, original surveys, or unique analysis are cited at higher rates than pages aggregating publicly available information.
- **Clear entity signals:** Pages from brands with strong Knowledge Graph presence and consistent entity representation are favoured.
- **Answer-first structure:** Content that states the main finding or answer in the first sentence of each section is retrieved more reliably than content that builds to a conclusion.
- **Specific, granular answers:** Content addressing precise questions ("What is the average latency for real-time analytics pipelines in financial services?") outperforms broad topic pages ("Everything you need to know about analytics").

## The zero-click reality: measuring what matters

AI Overviews continue to drive zero-click behaviour, particularly for informational queries where the AI provides a comprehensive answer. Some analyses indicate CTR declines of up to 61% for traditional organic results on AI Overview pages.

This does not mean search is less valuable. It means the value has shifted.

**New metrics that matter:**

| Metric | Why it matters |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Citation frequency** | How often your brand appears in AI-generated answers across target queries |
| **Citation accuracy** | Whether your brand is described correctly when cited |
| **Brand query volume** | AI citations drive branded search  -  users who see your brand in an AI answer search for you directly |
| **Expert Advice inclusion** | Whether your content appears in the dedicated Expert Advice block |
| **Preview card CTR** | Click-through rates from inline citation hover cards |

The shift from "traffic from search" to "visibility in search" requires new measurement frameworks. Monthly citation audits across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Claude are now as important as rank tracking.

## GEO strategy for the inline citation era

### 1. Restructure content for answer-first retrieval

Every section of every target page should begin with the core answer or finding in the first sentence. Supporting context, methodology, and nuance follow. This is not "dumbing down" content  -  it is structuring it for how AI systems retrieve and cite.

AI retrieval systems evaluate the first 1–3 sentences of each section for relevance. If your main point is buried in paragraph three, it will not be retrieved  -  regardless of quality.

### 2. Prioritise first-hand expertise and original data

The Expert Advice block rewards content that only your organisation can produce. Proprietary benchmarks, case study outcomes (anonymised where necessary), methodology documentation, and practitioner perspectives are the content types most likely to earn Expert Advice citations.

Generic content  -  "10 tips for better cybersecurity" or "what is cloud computing?"  -  is increasingly treated as commodity and excluded from citation.

### 3. Build entity authority for preview card recognition

When users hover over your citation, your brand name and page title appear. Investing in entity SEO  -  Organisation schema, Wikidata presence, consistent brand naming, external authority citations  -  directly affects whether that hover converts to a click.

### 4. Implement structured data for AI consumption

FAQPage schema, HowTo schema, and comprehensive Organisation schema with `sameAs` links are the structured data types most consistently used by AI systems. These are not new recommendations, but their importance has increased with inline citations.

### 5. Create content for conversational queries

With multimodal, longer-form search inputs, content should target specific questions at the granularity users actually ask. "How do I migrate from ELK to OpenSearch without downtime?" is a better content target than "OpenSearch migration guide."

## What this means for enterprise marketing teams

The May 2026 changes are not a reason to abandon SEO. Traditional organic search still drives significant discovery traffic, particularly for transactional and navigational queries. But informational queries  -  the top of the funnel  -  are increasingly answered by AI before users click anything.

**The practical implication:** enterprise brands need two parallel visibility programmes running simultaneously.

**Traditional SEO** optimises for ranking, click-through, and on-site conversion. It remains the primary driver for transactional intent.

**GEO** optimises for citation, brand accuracy, and authority signals across AI surfaces. It protects the top of the funnel from becoming invisible.

These are not competing priorities. They share technical foundations  -  structured data, entity clarity, content quality. The brands that implement both will capture visibility across both traditional and AI-mediated discovery.

**What is the difference between SEO and GEO?**
SEO optimises for ranking in traditional search results. GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) optimises for citation and accurate representation in AI-generated answers  -  Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude. Both share technical foundations (structured data, content quality, entity clarity) but target different visibility surfaces.

**Will AI Overviews eliminate organic search traffic?**
Not entirely. Transactional and navigational queries still drive significant click-through. Informational queries  -  particularly those where AI provides a complete answer  -  are seeing reduced CTR. The shift is from "all traffic through clicks" to "some visibility through citations, some through clicks."

**How do I get cited in Google's Expert Advice blocks?**
Publish content with demonstrable first-hand experience: proprietary data, case study outcomes, methodology documentation, and practitioner perspectives. Generic, aggregated content is systematically excluded. Build author credibility signals and ensure your content clearly communicates expertise.
